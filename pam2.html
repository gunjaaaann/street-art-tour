Stop 2

At this stop, Pam the Bird, further exemplifies how street art enlivens industrial monotony of a rail corridor and transforms routine urban movement into playful engagement. Its bright, cartoon-like form - playful yet mischievously placed invites what Cooper & Sandlin (2019) describe as spontaneous urban play, drawing compelling connections between street art and urban landscape. Likewise, The Project (2024) also observes how “spot the bird” has become a light-hearted pastime for commuters, highlighting the Pam’s appeal across audiences.

Research on graffiti often emphasises site specificity, suggesting that artworks benefit from their surroundings (Chmielewska 2007). However, Pam demonstrates that graffiti is not merely site-responsive but site-generative, contributing to a site by actively shaping the experience of passerby. This participatory encounter underscores how street art redistributes aesthetic experience from fixed, institutional settings to the flows of everyday travel.
